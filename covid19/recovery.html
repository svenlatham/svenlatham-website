Recovery by Country

This document is tracking recovery methods/protocols by country (typically EU). The goal is to track major events, and local data as the countries lift their respective restrictions.

Last updated 19/4. Note this is based on Wikipedia, so re-research before quoting!

France

Wikipedia 
2020-03-12 Announcement that schools would close from 16/3
2020-03-13 Closure of all non-essential public places (restaurants, cinemas, etc).
2020-03-16 Mandatory home confinement for 15 days starting noon 17/3
2020-03-27 Extension of home confinement until 15/4.
2020-04-08 Further extension
2020-04-13 Lockdown to be continued until 11 May.

Germany

Wikipedia 
2020-01-27 First case confirmed
2020-03-09 First death
2020-03-13 Protection stage - School & kindergarten closures.
2020-03-15 Borders to five neighbouring countries closed.
2020-03-22 National curfew; commute to work, sports and shopping only. Not more than two people.
2020-03-31 Jena first larger city to oblige wearing of masks.
2020-04-07 Launch of Corona-Datenspende (app) for voluntary use to monitor spread of virus. 50,000 installed the same day.
2020-04-20 (announced 15/4) Shops with retail space up to 800sq.m to reopen, + bookshops, bike stores and car dealerships.
2020-04-27 Face masks become compulsory, at least on public transport (rollout date by state varies)
2020-05-03 (annc 15/4) Restrictions on movements remain in place until at least this date.
2020-05-04 (annc 15/4) Schools to re-open. Hair salons to open (under strict rules).
2020-08-31 (annc 15/4) No large cultural events before this date.

Belgium

Wikipedia 
2020-02-04 First case detected
2020-03-10 Govt advise against indoor events with more than 1000 people.
2020-03-11 First death on Belgian territory
2020-03-13 (annc 12/3) Govt orders closure of schools, discos, cafes and restaurants.
2020-03-18 (annc 17/3) Non-essential travel prohibited. Non-essential shops to close. Gatherings banned.
2020-03-20 Borders closed to non-essential travel.
2020-05-03 (annc 27/3; 15/4) Measures extended to this date

Netherlands

Wikipedia 
2020-02-27 First case detected
2020-03-06 First death reported.
2020-03-09 Citizens asked to stop shaking hands. Citizens in Noord-Brabant to work from home where possible.
2020-03-10 In Noord-Brabant, larger events cancelled.
2020-03-12 All meetings 100+ people cancelled. Work encouraged from home.
2020-03-15 Schools closed. Cafes, restaurants, sports clubs, saunas, sex clubs and coffeeshops to close.
2020-03-17 Schools to stay open only for final year students' exams.
2020-03-23 Stricter social distancing announced.
2020-03-24 Final exams cancelled.
2020-04-28 (annc 31/3) All measures extended.
